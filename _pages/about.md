## About me

I am a senior at [Carnegie Mellon University](https://www.cmu.edu/) majoring in `Computer Science` (System and Network Concentration) with an additional concentration in `Robotics`. I'm a member of [Catalyst Group](https://catalyst.cs.cmu.edu), advised by Prof. [Todd C. Mowry](https://www.toddcmowry.org).

## Efficient LLM

My research is dedicated to enhancing the efficiency and long-context capacity of LLMs through co-optimization of algorithms
and underlying parallel computer systems. Outside CMU, I work on efficient LLMs and am fortunate to be mentored by Prof. [Song Han](https://hanlab.mit.edu/songhan) and Research Scientist [Han Cai](https://han-cai.github.io) at MIT HAN Lab and Nvidia.

## Robotics

Before I started doing research on LLMs, I spent 1.5 years of my undergraduate on robotics research in [DROP Lab](https://www.ri.cmu.edu/robotics-groups/drop-deep-robot-optical-perception-lab/) at CMU where I was mentored by Prof. [Matthew Johnson-Roberson](https://computing.vanderbilt.edu/bio/matthew-johnson-roberson/) (now Dean of CCC at Vanderbilt University) and Prof. [Weiming Zhi](https://www.weimingzhi.com). I've worked with robots such as robot manipulator(s), quadruped, and humanoid, and my main focus in robotics was robotic perception for manipulation and robot foundation models.
